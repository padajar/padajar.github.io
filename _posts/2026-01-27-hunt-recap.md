## puzzles

#### this puzzle has been here the whole time (bubble cove, feeder)

hooooly cow what an incredible puzzle to see to start the hunt. i *immediately* head to it with some other dropout nerds. had a small miststep to start (totally thought the first diagram was referencing [lisa gilroy as ellen degeneres in jaws](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/zsmxkpCpUK8)), but we quickly got past that. the sam reich cameo was absolutely *incredible* to boot. in hindsight, could have solved it sooner, but so it goes with puzzles. deeply enjoyed and also immediately forwarded to *all* the people i know who are into dropout[^1] (unlocked fri 1:19pm, solved fri 2:16pm.)

## nes 

i started doing puzzlehunts in 2019 with N3XT (a group of folks from 3W/3E); we were excited to solve 30 puzzles and a meta. the next year, we merged with another next house team (TWTW from 2W/3W) to form  a next house superteam‚Ñ¢ named NES that managed to solve a whopping 64 puzzles. we joined forces with 2MysterE (2E) in 2021 amidst the pandemic to become a supersuperteam‚Ñ¢, and to our complete surprise, we solved 174 puzzles and placed 15th. 2022 was 18th, 2023 10th, 2024 6th/8th[^2], 2025 we *finished* and got ‚Äú4th‚Äù,[^a] and 2026 4th for *real*. even though i was only a team member during the early years, it‚Äôs been wild to see how far we‚Äôve come. i‚Äôm still somewhat shocked when other people recognize the name of our team. it‚Äôs weird hearing other people say that they‚Äôre rooting for us to *win* over the next several years. in my head, we‚Äôre still just a group of friends that gets together for puzzles during IAP, but of course, we‚Äôre now 70 people who can finish whole mystery hunts. 

in 2023, i joined the ‚Äúsheriffs‚Äù of nes, and started helping out with various organizational things. sign-up forms, trying to improve puzzle documentation and communication and team coordination, and, perhaps most saliently for team members, being one of the two strategy captains. this means i help run our State Of The Solves,[^3] try and keep apprised of how our hunt progress is going at a high level, and doing things to vaguely move our solving-power towards places where it‚Äôs most needed. i don‚Äôt think any single one of us is in ‚Äúcharge‚Äù of nes, but i think during hunt, me + katie (our other strategy captain) end up making many of these high-level decisions in the moment.

this is only the second year that NES has had strategy captains, and there‚Äôs certainly a lot that we‚Äôre still figuring out how to do. some reflections from this year:

* i think we did a pretty good job at managing the unlock queue this year. my opinion here is largely because the puzzle width[^4] was less of a constraint this year, and so we had to make fewer ‚Äúhard‚Äù decisions on what to unlock and where. we started hunt with a width (‚Äúpuzzmon capacity‚Äù) of 8, a width that later increased to 10 (and then 12, and then 15). more importantly, unlocking Dimension rounds later in the hunt didn‚Äôt count towards that width, effectively doubling our puzzle width (or more). i also don‚Äôt think we ever got to a point where it felt like there were too *many* puzzles open ‚Äî maybe i should do some stats on our width throughout the hunt. 

  this stands in contrast to last year, where i feel like we felt much more constrained by the number of puzzles that we had unlocked at all times, and so it made for a *very* different hunt experience (and lots of people wanting something unlocked at all times). 

* a good amount of my time was spent trying to keep track of the state of our progress. at least 30 minutes around every dinner was spent prepping/doing state of the solve. and i think almost all of sunday was spent coordinating hints, trying to see where we needed more solves, and guiding people towards those places. 

  to be clear, i do enjoy the tradeoff ‚Äî it‚Äôs nice to be able to put on an organizing hat, to think through team strategies, and also just to check in on people and try and help them enjoy hunt. but just noting to myself that objectively, i spend way less time during mystery hunt actually *doing* puzzles than many other people on my team.[^b]

* making decisions once you are more tired is always very hard. as hunt goes on, and you‚Äôve been staring at computers for 30 hours, stuck on incredibly hard puzzles, one isn‚Äôt always in the best state of mind for things. i don‚Äôt think that we did anything *wrong*, per se, but i think there are certainly ways in which i could have said things better, done things with more tact, etc etc. this especially is relevant to‚Ä¶

* figuring out the right way to encourage people to use hints.[^5] i sent the below paragraphs into ``#strategy-discussion`` before the hunt talking about our philosophy towards hints and free-solves:

  > For hints and free-solves: we'll be keeping the same strategy that we've used in previous years. In essence, this strategy is one of "let's try our best to help people enjoy puzzling". We'll be using the hint system as it opens (and will have a channel for managing hints,[^6] since we usually can only have one submitted at a time). For free-solves, our rule of thumb has been that if someone's excited/actively working on a puzzle, we avoid free-solving as much as possible. We try to have dialogue between meta captains / people working on puzzle / strategy captains to see whether this is one that would help our progress if we indeed could free-solve, and always look for an "ok" from the relevant solvers that they're ok with their puzzle being free-solved. 
  >
  > There's more "aggressive" versions of each of these strategies. The most direct one (that I've heard some other teams use) is that their strategy captain/meta captain equivalents will free-solve puzzles once they decide that they need it / progress would be too slow on it, even if there might be some excitement about it. That "do we need it" can also be done pretty aggressively, in a way that we haven't done before. (Hints wise, I think it's a little hard to be more "winning-oriented" than what we're doing now, besides lowering the level of "stuck" where people ask hints. but also like . that's no fun)

  of course, free-solves didn‚Äôt end up being relevant this hunt, but hints very much were. our team didn‚Äôt unlock the ability to hint until around 7pm on saturday, and figuring out the right ways to use hints on sunday was‚Ä¶ difficult. in a sense, there‚Äôs four balancing priorities:

  1. people want to finish the hunt,
  2. people want to have the joy of figuring out a puzzle, 
  3. people want to be done with a puzzle where they feel completely stuck and where nothing‚Äôs moving, and
  4. people don‚Äôt want to sit around doing nothing.

  objectively, we didn‚Äôt hint very much: 9 hints requested (with 2 follow ups for clarification),[^c] and one in-person nudge on the alphabet meta. but subjectively, it did feel like we ended up being fairly aggressive in terms of encouraging people to hint, particularly on sunday. (i‚Äôm especially thinking about 2 particular feeders in the *glitch* round that probably could have been done sans hint with another few hours of puzzle time.) 

  our (as in, me + katie, NES‚Äô other strategy captain) in-the-moment decisions boiled down to:

  1. our width was quite small (3 puzzles), meaning lots of people on very few puzzles,

  2. the glitch meta being an unknown difficulty, and us needing more puzzles to unlock it (at 5/9 solved we thought we‚Äôd need 1 more, but then we got to 6/9 and still needed another),

  3. not knowing if a metameta came after the glitch meta, and

  4. cardinality encouraging us to be aggressive with hints.

  and so on balance, items 1, 3, and 4 made us decide that hints were worth it, and led to a good amount of encouraging to hint after it seemed like people weren‚Äôt making progress, but still leaving it up to the puzzle-doers to actually write up/submit a hint. 

  in hindsight, we likely could have finished the hunt without hints on those two puzzles, and so the fact that we encouraged hinting when those hints might otherwise not have been taken doesn‚Äôt feel great. i do think that, on net, taking those hints was a right decision in that moment. not necessarily the only one, of course. but so goes trying to make any choice.

* where does NES go from here? like i said above ‚Äî i‚Äôve heard multiple people (not on NES) say that they‚Äôre rooting for NES to win hunt sometime in the next several years. we‚Äôre somewhere near the top (if not first)[^7] on a ranking that‚Äôs a combination of {recent mit graduates/low in age, does well in hunts}. about half of the people that filled out our census poll were a class of 2020/2021/2022. 

  for the first time ever, our sign-up form had a survey to collect people‚Äôs thoughts on winning. by and large, people don‚Äôt feel ready yet, but are excited to do the things that would help them feel ready (i.e., writing more puzzles) ‚Äî a few years ago, we started writing a NES hunt, and though momentum sputtered, we‚Äôre planning to get back on that this year with some internal potlucks and whatnot. 

  there seems to be a decent amount of excitement to someday actually win. if all goes well, of starting to actively try and win come 2028+. of course, this is all thinking years in advance, and we‚Äôll see how trying to write potlucks and hunts goes, and people would have to want to do things when we get there, and we‚Äôd have to actually *win* (certainly no guarantees on that), but there‚Äôs are worlds where we win in the next 5‚Äì10 years that aren‚Äôt very farfetched.
  
  it‚Äôs a bit weird to think about it all. people make such a big deal about winning hunt, and writing the next one, in a way that feels a bit unsustainable as a practice. (i‚Äôve heard some stories of people that took a year sabbatical from their jobs just to write hunt, though i doubt that‚Äôs something i‚Äôll be able to do.) sure, it‚Äôs incredibly grand in scale and there are thousands and thousands of people hours needed to write and test hundreds of puzzles. but also, between the ESP and theater backgrounds of our leadership team, i feel like we‚Äôd be able to figure it out.

as always, shoutouts to all the sheriffs (Katie, Mary, Michael, Mihir, Mindren, Zach) for being such great people to run NES with, and for putting so much into this team (non-exhaustive list of things done this year: swag, NESbot, thinking about running hunts/helping people practice writing puzzles, hauling snacks around, discussions on strategies and team goals, having a crate of NES items year-round in their house, coordinating subteams on NES to help with other things, and making NES a lovely place). 

here‚Äôs to more NES. no end (in) sight :) 

## hunt overall

various other observations on this year‚Äôs hunt. 

- so many cool physical objects. *puzzmon the card game* was incredible, both as a game and in terms of production value. so was the faux-tamagotchi and the hot sauce puzzle (while not for me, i‚Äôm sure it‚Äôs somebody‚Äôs idea of fun)

- the scavenger hunt giving RP which was used to unlock puzzles was a great way of making scavenger tasks feel more organic and necessary, rather than something that you only use once. they certainly fell off in terms of usefulness as time went on, but i think that is ok

- as someone who didn‚Äôt go to events, the concept of having many smaller events throughout is super interesting. it‚Äôs certainly more of an onus for smaller teams, but it let a lot more people engage in events than in most hunt years. good or bad, depending on whatever a (hunting) team‚Äôs goal is (and i do think it is a net good for the overall experience of IRL teams)

- i quite enjoyed the gimmicks of various dimension rounds, particularly *land of no name* (though i didn‚Äôt solve anything there) and *atlas*. do hope to go back to the latter and check out rounds i didn‚Äôt do

- i‚Äôm realizing that i enjoy hunt stories more when they have lots of real-life interaction. i understand why the choices get made to not do this, and that hunts without a lot of real-life interaction have their own enjoyable things, but i think i have enough mystery hunts under my belt to feel more confident in the existence of this opinion
